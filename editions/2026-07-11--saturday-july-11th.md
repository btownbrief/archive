## Weather & Weekend Rundown:

One day late but it’s well, welllll worth it. Here I am at 4:21 a.m., finishing up, even though we have coffee at 10! Lots of work, we’ll talk more about that later. Anyway, this weekend is about as clean as July gets. Friday shook off the last of the clouds, and Saturday might be the best day of the whole stretch with wall to wall sun, low humidity, another 81, ideal for a market run in the morning and a night at the ballpark. Sunday stays sunny and dry and nudges up to 84. Soak it up now, because early next week turns genuinely hot, with Monday flirting with 90 and Tuesday looking like mid 90s before the next shower chance creeps in Tuesday night.

Saturday's lineup really capitalizes on that gorgeous forecast, and the sports slate alone is huge. The headline is [Vermont Green FC's women hosting Asheville City SC in the USL W League National Semifinal](https://vermontgreenfc.com/2026-schedule-women/) at 7 PM, after playoff wins over Long Island and Eagle FC. Fair warning, [tickets for the match at UVM's Virtue Field were listed as sold out](https://tickets.vermontgreenfc.com/event/2026-uslw-national-semifinal-rzc35t) as of Friday, so if you're not holding one already, find a friend who is. [Green Mountain Roller Derby's Summer Jam](https://www.facebook.com/events/1667667897618465) brings Vermont's Grade A Fancy up against the Bay State Brawlers at the Champlain Valley Expo, 4 to 8. The [Lake Monsters game goes Star Wars with fireworks after](https://www.meetup.com/forever-38/events/314740568/), and the Forever 38 group has reserved grandstand seating from 6 to 10 if you want built in company. Soccer watchers can catch [Norway against England in the World Cup quarterfinal at Citizen Cider](https://www.helloburlingtonvt.com/event/world-cup-watch-party/7715/), 5 to 7:30 with 5 dollar pours and 10 dollar smashburgers.

Saturday daytime belongs to the morning people. Our [BTown Coffee Club](https://www.meetup.com/burlington-social-activites-group/events/315354166/) settles into Zero Gravity from 10 to 12 as always, usually 15 to 30 of us, come find the big table. The [Burlington Farmers Market](https://community.sevendaysvt.com/event/burlington-farmers-market-45042389) fills 345 Pine Street with 70 plus vendors from 9 to 2, garden people get the [annual plant sale at the UVM Hort Farm](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/503697) in South Burlington from 10 to 1, and the [Winooski Gardening Group](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/503583) spruces up the rotary from 8:30 to 11 if you'd rather dig than shop. Car folks have a [charity Cars and Coffee at OnLogic](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/charity-cars-coffee-onlogic-registration-1993550831201) from 7:30 to 9:30 expecting 75 to 100 plus cars, free with donations going to local charities. The truly ambitious can preregister for [Pasture Songs at Shelburne Farms](https://community.sevendaysvt.com/event/pasture-songs-a-creative-soundmapping-experience-45269858), a sunrise soundmapping experience that really does run 4:30 to 6:30 in the morning. Downtown, [Quartet Hops](https://community.sevendaysvt.com/event/quartet-hops-45229659) drops festival string quartets somewhere new on the Marketplace at the top of every hour from 10 to 4, free, while [Spotlight Vermont 250](https://www.helloburlingtonvt.com/event/spotlight-vermont-250/7569/) packs City Hall Park with 30 plus museums and historical societies from 11 to 2 for the state's 250th. That should be a really, really fun one to add to your weekend plans. Then, [Mad Pride marches its 10th anniversary](https://www.helloburlingtonvt.com/event/mad-pride-10th-anniversary-march-and-celebration/7710/) down Church Street at noon with a free festival back at the UU Church from 1 to 3. Families can do the [hike and ice cream combo at Red Rocks Park](https://www.facebook.com/events/1530120192134686) from 12 to 2, and readers can bring "Atmosphere" by Taylor Jenkins Reid to the [Burlington Brewery Book Club at Burlington Beer Company](https://www.meetup.com/burlington-brewery-book-club/events/314353888/) from 2 to 4.

Saturday night keeps rolling. [VT Cider Lab throws its three year anniversary party](https://www.facebook.com/events/1011877981234471) in Essex, open 1 to 9 with free admission and live music on the green from 4 to 9, Lloyd Tyler then King Me. [Made In Iron celebrates 20 years](https://www.helloburlingtonvt.com/event/made-in-iron/7051/) of being Vermont's Iron Maiden tribute with an all ages [8 PM show at Higher Ground, 15 dollars in advance](https://highergroundmusic.com/events/made-in-iron/). And Essex Junction gets a late one with [Luau Night at On Tap](https://www.facebook.com/events/4464864913768841), The Kwame Vibe spinning 90s to now from 9 to midnight.

Sunday winds down easy. Early birds can hit [free lululemon yoga at Waterfront Park](https://www.helloburlingtonvt.com/event/lululemon-summer-series-%7c-yoga-at-waterfront-park/7688/) from 8 to 9, then [free chamber music at the BCA Center](https://www.helloburlingtonvt.com/event/sunday-classical%3a-the-green-mountain-chamber-music-festival/7399/) from 10 to 11 as the festival's eight fellows play the gallery. The [Lake Monsters close the weekend with Make-A-Wish Night](https://www.helloburlingtonvt.com/event/vermont-lake-monsters-make-a-wish-night/7228/) at 5:05, celebrating local wish families, 12 for adults and 6 for kids.

#### And the City Guide grew up. With 16 new INCREDIBLE pages!

The guide used to be a list of good spots. Now I created a live picture of the city that updates itself all day long. **[Open Right Now](https://guide.btownbrief.com/restaurants.html)** knows the real hours of 281 restaurants and bars, so at 10:40 on a Tuesday night it can tell you who is still serving food. **[Events](https://guide.btownbrief.com/events.html)** pulls from twenty-five different sources so that you don’t have to check twenty-five different sources. **[Tonight’s Sunset](https://guide.btownbrief.com/sunset.html)** scores the sky out of 10, tells you when to leave the house, and tells you where to stand. And **[Burlington Right Now](https://guide.btownbrief.com/weather.html)** has the lake temperature, which beaches are open today, and whether it’s worth going outside at all.

There’s more underneath. And I created every single one of these! **[Food & Drink Deals](https://guide.btownbrief.com/deals.html)** has a growing list of happy hours, daily specials and cheap-night ideas. **[Burlington Pulse](https://guide.btownbrief.com/chatter.html)** shows what the town is talking about right now, with rise-and-fall arrows on each topic, so you can watch something catch fire before it ever reaches the news. Then there’s the newest **[jobs](https://guide.btownbrief.com/jobs.html)** posted around town this week, every **[property manager](https://guide.btownbrief.com/housing.html)** in Burlington so you can stop refreshing Zillow at midnight, **[volunteer shifts](https://guide.btownbrief.com/volunteer.html)** sorted by how much time you’ve actually got, and **[Since You Checked](https://guide.btownbrief.com/changes.html)**, which does exactly one job… to tell you what changed in Burlington since the last time you looked.

[New boardwalk links South Burlington streets through City Center Park](https://www.wcax.com/2026/07/07/new-boardwalk-links-south-burlington-streets-through-city-center-park/)

❝

"This is greenery and forest that people so badly need as a break from streets and sidewalks. And dogs like it, too." per WCAX

Phase 2 of City Center Park is officially done, with the new shared-use boardwalk tying together Barrett, Iby, Market and Garden streets. It doubles as part of the Safe Routes to School network, and the path now has a new main sign on Market Street plus signage on native birds, wetlands and a story walk. Worth a wander this weekend while the weather is behaving.

[New Chittenden County recycling facility to remain on track after waste district recovers $2.27 million stolen in scam](https://vtdigger.org/2026/07/07/new-chittenden-county-recycling-facility-to-remain-on-track-after-waste-district-recovers-2-27-million-stolen-in-scam/)

❝

"We were in shock, and it was devastating," she said of learning that the district had sent a large amount of money to a fraudulent account. "It was horrible, and there's no manual for this." per VTDigger

Scammers posing as the project's construction company made off with more than 3 million dollars in January, and the FBI managed to claw back 2.27 million of it. That keeps the new recycling plant, funded largely by the 22 million dollar bond county voters passed in 2022, on schedule for a January testing phase and a spring 2027 opening. Executive director Sarah Reeves' takeaway is worth repeating, report scams immediately and skip the shame, because speed is what made the recovery possible.

[Poll: Most young Vermonters say they're likely to leave state amid affordability concerns](https://vtdigger.org/2026/07/08/poll-most-young-vermonters-say-theyre-likely-to-leave-state-amid-affordability-concerns/)

❝

"Nearly two-thirds of Vermonters ages 18 to 34 say they're likely to leave the state within five years in search of adventure or a cheaper place to live, according to a poll from the University of New Hampshire." per VTDigger

That 63 percent figure is the highest of any New England state surveyed, more than double New Hampshire's 28 percent, and housing costs and taxes are the main drivers. Researchers note a real boomerang effect of natives returning later as higher earners, and one analyst points out that young people are always the most mobile group in any poll. Still, with 86 percent of Vermonters calling the state at least somewhat unaffordable, the workforce math gets uglier every year this holds.

[The Long Road Taken to Open Burlington's Champlain Parkway](https://www.sevendaysvt.com/news/constructiondevelopment/the-long-road-taken-to-open-burlingtons-champlain-parkway/)

❝

"Over the course of 61 years, the parkway has been reimagined and redesigned a dozen times to meet the demands of South End residents; rerouted to avoid a Superfund site; and delayed by environmental studies, 10 lawsuits, and shifting federal, state and city priorities." per Seven Days

The definitive history of how a 1960s four-lane highway dream became an 84 million dollar, 2.8 mile, 25 mile per hour parkway. Former public works director Steve Goodkind calls the finished road "a monument to ignorance" while the current administration frames six decades of redesigns as the public process working. The part to watch is the final piece, the proposed 55 million dollar Railyard Enterprise Project connecting Pine to Battery Street, which has only 20 million lined up and hinges on land negotiations with Vermont Rail System that should get a city council update this summer.

**[Vermont bodyworker built a bustling practice despite mounting allegations](https://www.vermontpublic.org/local-news/2026-07-08/vermont-bodyworker-built-bustling-practice-despite-mounting-allegations)**

❝

"Garcia built his business in a state that has some of the loosest regulations for bodyworkers and massage therapists in the country. Vermont is one of a handful of states that does not require bodyworkers to demonstrate any certifications or competency to practice, even though state data indicates that the profession has a high rate of complaints for sexual misconduct." per Vermont Public

Gregory Garcia ran Bodywork Architectures out of the Chace Mill, claimed more than 600 clients, and performed an "internal work" technique on women that the field's own credentialing bodies call flatly out of bounds. Because he had never registered with the state, regulators initially could only fine him 1,000 dollars for unauthorized practice, and it took two years from the first complaint before an administrative judge upheld the state's refusal to let him practice at all. He is appealing again, and the piece will fuel the licensure debate that lawmakers have so far sidestepped in favor of a lighter registration rule for bodywork establishments.

**[Content creator 'Planet Hank' sues Vermont attorney general over AI video probe](https://vtdigger.org/2026/07/09/content-creator-planet-hank-sues-vermont-attorney-general-over-ai-video-probe/)**

❝

"The new legislation requires that artificially generated media that 'injures the reputation of a political candidate' carry a disclosure stating the media is fake, if it is published within 90 days of an election." per VTDigger

Vermont's brand-new AI election law is getting its first real courtroom test, and the test case is a video of Rep. Becca Balint being dropped into a dumpster by an excavator. Brattleboro streamer Hank Poitras says the video is obvious satire protected by the First Amendment, while the attorney general's office says all it wants is a disclosure caption and offered to drop the probe if he adds one. With the August 11 primary inside the law's 90 day window, a federal judge may end up deciding how far Vermont can go in policing fake political media just as the election heats up.

**[Why beaches close due to cyanobacteria, algae blooms](https://www.mychamplainvalley.com/news/local-news/vermont/north-beach-among-burlington-beaches-closed-due-to-algae-blooms/)**

❝

"Closures indicate that Cyanobacteria blooms are present in 'levels potentially dangerous for health', according to the Burlington Parks, Recreation and Waterfront beach closure tracker." per Local 22/44

With a hot, sunny weekend on deck, this is your public service announcement. No Burlington beaches are closed right now, but several have opened and closed already this season, and staff check for blooms daily from Memorial Day to Labor Day. The blooms can look like pea soup or spilled paint and can cause rashes, stomach trouble, and worse, so give the city's beach closure tracker a ten second glance before you commit to a swim. Or just [check my site!](https://guide.btownbrief.com/)

**[Switchback Brewing Co. adds KRUSH N.A. IPA to year-round lineup](https://vermontbiz.com/news/2026/july/08/switchback-brewing-co-adds-krush-na-ipa-year-round-lineup)**

**[Burlington Considers 15 Applicants for Police Chief](https://www.sevendaysvt.com/news/burlington-considers-15-applicants-for-police-chief/)**

## Vermont Green FC Men Secure Division Title as Women Prepare for National Semifinal

Vermont Green FC has officially clinched back-to-back titles as the [2026 USL2 Northeast Division Champions](https://vermontgreenfc.com/vermont-green-are-2026-usl2-northeast-division-champs/) following a decisive victory. The squad secured the trophy by hitting 37 points on the season with a commanding [6-2 win over Connecticut Rush](https://vermontgreenfc.com/usl2-match-report-vermont-green-6-2-connecticut-rush/). The match was highlighted by a record-breaking performance from striker Jeremy Francou, who netted a single-match club record of five goals. Samson Kpardeh added the sixth goal, while Alex Hall provided a hat-trick of assists to solidify the historic win.

Following their championship-clinching match, the Boys in Green concluded their regular season with a [4-2 victory against the Western Mass. Pioneers](https://vermontgreenfc.com/usl2-match-report-vermont-green-4-2-western-mass-pioneers/). This road win cemented a second straight undefeated regular season with a 13W-1D-0L record, extending head coach Chris Taylor’s unbeaten streak to 37 matches. Francou finished the regular season with 15 goals, while Will Sawin notably scored a goal off the bench in an eventful appearance. The team will now look ahead as [USL2 Playoffs return to Virtue Field](https://vermontgreenfc.com/usl2-playoffs-are-coming-to-virtue-field-tickets-on-sale-monday/), with Vermont Green set to host an Eastern Conference Quarterfinals doubleheader on Friday, July 17, and the Eastern Conference Semifinal on Sunday, July 19.

On the women’s side, the undefeated Vermont Green FC squad is gearing up for a monumental showdown in the [USLW National Semifinal against Asheville City SC](https://vermontgreenfc.com/uslw-playoff-match-preview-vermont-green-vs-asheville-city-sc/). The Green, who have scored 36 goals this summer without conceding a single one in their last 280 minutes of postseason play, will face the Southern Conference Champions in a sold-out clash. The women's team will host this highly anticipated matchup at Virtue Field this Saturday.

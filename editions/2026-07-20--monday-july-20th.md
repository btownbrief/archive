## Weather & Monday Update:

If you like your summer days sunny and your humidity low, soak up Monday while it lasts. Today is the gem of the stretch, mostly sunny with a high right around 83, a light southwest breeze, and air quality back in the good range after a hazy weekend. Tuesday starts decent but turns busier, with humidity spiking, showers and thunderstorms likely by mid afternoon, gusts that could push past 30 mph, and a chance that wildfire smoke sneaks back in on the south wind. Wednesday stays unsettled with showers likely through the morning and storm chances into the afternoon before things dry out at night. Thursday is the reward, sunny and comfortable with a high near 79 and cool sleeping weather behind it. If you have outdoor plans Tuesday evening or Wednesday morning, keep half an eye on the radar.

Monday itself eases you into the week. Runners can lace up for [Skirack's Run Club](https://www.facebook.com/events/998796846092907/) at 5:45, a casual paced community run out of the Main Street shop that shoots for 3 to 5 miles with groups by pace. Riders get the same energy in Williston, where Catamount hosts a [Novice Monday group mountain bike ride](https://www.facebook.com/events/1481766713594488/) from 6 to 8. If your speed is quieter, the [Monday Night Open Mic](https://community.sevendaysvt.com/event/monday-night-open-mic-44612935) at Pearl Street Pub in Essex Junction gets going at 6, and parents can start the day early with the free [Family Concert Series](https://community.sevendaysvt.com/event/family-concert-series-45229838) at Dorothy Alling Memorial Library in Williston at 11:15, where Green Mountain Chamber Music Festival students play a short set with a sweet treat after. And from our corner, our group's [Sunset Paddle at Shelburne Bay](https://www.meetup.com/burlington-social-activites-group/events/315733837/) launches at 7 tonight. Bring a kayak or board, catch the sunset from the water, and come say hi.

Tuesday is the big ticket day. [Gillian Welch and David Rawlings ($56)](https://www.helloburlingtonvt.com/event/gillian-welch-and-david-rawlings/7622/) play the Ben & Jerry's Concerts on the Green at Shelburne Museum at 7, with kids 12 and under free on the lawn. Earlier in the afternoon, the Old North End celebrates the [Pomeroy Park playground grand opening](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/505027) from 2 to 3 with a ribbon cutting alongside the mayor. Curious minds can drop into [Where is AI in 2026 and Where is it Going?](https://www.meetup.com/ai-safety-awareness-group-burlington/events/315609672/) at Fletcher Free Library at 5:30, a free discussion workshop run through the AI Safety Awareness Meetup where the talking is the point. Night owls can close it out on the waterfront with Swiss psych funk outfit [L'Eclair with Two Sev and DJ Rice Pilaf](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/leclair-tickets-1988442610375) at Foam Brewers at 8:30, or two step into [Honky Tonk Tuesday ($10)](https://community.sevendaysvt.com/event/honky-tonk-tuesday-with-queen-city-cut-ups-45314403) at Radio Bean at 8:30.

Wednesday is stacked even with the clouds hanging around. The monster show is [Trombone Shorty with JJ Grey & Mofro](https://highergroundmusic.com/events/trombone-shorty/) on the Green at Shelburne Museum at 7, though fair warning, Higher Ground lists it as sold out, so it is waitlist or ticket exchange territory now. Colchester counters with the [Sam Mazza's Summer Concert Series featuring B-Town](https://www.facebook.com/events/854888383688465/) from 6 to 8 at the farm market on Lavigne Road. Freelancers and remote workers can talk shop at [After Hours at Foam Brewers](https://www.facebook.com/events/1001403922689756/), a casual 21+ networking evening at 6. The [Craftsbury Chamber Players](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/505568) bring their summer season to the Elley-Long Music Center in Colchester at 7:30 with students $10 and kids free, and [GYMSHORTS with POLKAROBICS, the Eyetraps, and the Skizm](https://community.sevendaysvt.com/event/gymshorts-polkarobics-the-eyetraps-the-skizm-45314405) turns Radio Bean loud at 8. Our own [Pick-up Basketball at Pomeroy Park](https://www.meetup.com/burlington-social-activites-group/events/315521331/) runs 5:30 to 7:30, so come out and run some full court games.

Thursday might be the best all around day of the week, sunny, 79, and absolutely stacked. The bricks host [Party On The Bricks](https://www.helloburlingtonvt.com/event/party-on-the-bricks/7524/) from 5:30 to 8, free on Church Street with the Fly-By Ride and music on the Opera Block. South Burlington answers with [SB Nite Out](https://www.facebook.com/events/1280713840312254/) at 5:30, free live music from A House on Fire plus a blanket giveaway for the first 500 through the gate. Williston gets [GARTH Live](https://community.sevendaysvt.com/event/maple-tree-place-summer-concert-series-garth-live-45316812), a high octane Garth Brooks tribute, at the Maple Tree Place Summer Concert Series at 6, and Shelburne Museum keeps its own streak going with the free [Shelburne Summer Nights](https://community.sevendaysvt.com/event/shelburne-summer-nights-45223065) from 5 to 7:30. Out at Isham Family Farm, loon biologist Eric Hanson gives a free [Loon Talk](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/503515) from 5:30 to 7:30, timely since the lake's loon pair hatched two chicks this summer. Animal people can paint their own companion at [Paint Your Pet](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/505266) at Queen City Cat Lounge from 6 to 8 (register by Wednesday), trivia types get [Music Bingo](https://www.facebook.com/events/4517729085142610/) at Sparky's in Essex Junction at 6:30, and film buffs can catch Kurosawa's [The Hidden Fortress](https://www.helloburlingtonvt.com/event/the-hidden-fortress/7699/) with the Vermont International Film Festival at 7. Comedy fans should grab [Molly Kearney ($25)](https://www-vermontcomedyclub-com.seatengine.com/events/136832) at Vermont Comedy Club at 7, the SNL alum in for one night. Later on, Winooski's Monkey House hosts [King Garbage with C.Shreve the Professor and 3rd Chamber ($10)](https://www.facebook.com/events/1327675472897309/), doors at 8. And if you feel like a short drive, the free [Sticks and Stones concert](https://www.helloburlingtonvt.com/event/summer-concert-series-sticks-and-stones/5741/) at Snow Farm Vineyard in South Hero runs 6:30 to 8:30, lawn chairs and sunset views encouraged.

## Local News (All Links Clickable)

[Shelburne Museum builds on its legacy with new Native American art center](https://www.wcax.com/2026/07/15/shelburne-museum-builds-its-legacy-with-new-native-american-art-center/)

❝

*"Every museum claims they're unique, but I like to say we really are," per WCAX.*

Shelburne Museum is putting up its 40th building, the Perry Center for Native American Art, its first new construction since 2013 and slated to open around summer 2027. The design has been shaped by input from about 120 representatives of Indigenous communities, a process in the works since 2008, and the center will house works from more than 300 tribal nations. If you want a taste of the campus before then, the museum's free Summer Nights series runs this Thursday evening.

[United States' 250th birthday, but just Vermont's 235th](https://www.charlottenewsvt.org/2026/07/09/united-states-250th-birthday-but-just-vermonts-235th/)

❝

*"We can learn that this is how democracy works. It's about courage, conviction and what we believe in," per Community News Service.*

While the country gears up for its semiquincentennial, Vermont's own founding story gets a fun retelling here. The state spent 14 years as an independent republic, coining its own money and running its own post, and its 1777 constitution was the first in North America to prohibit slavery and extend the vote to all male residents. The Ethan Allen Homestead Museum right here in Burlington features in the piece, worth a visit if the history itch strikes.

[Burlington City Council Approves Plan for Battery Storage System](https://www.sevendaysvt.com/news/burlington-city-council-approves-plan-for-battery-storage-system/)

❝

*"In this case, we're a municipal utility, so any benefits we see, our ratepayers will see, our community will see," per Seven Days.*

The council unanimously approved a 5 megawatt battery at the McNeil Generating Station, leased from Kearsarge Energy but controlled by Burlington Electric. The math is straightforward, BED's average hourly load is 37 megawatts and peaks hit 60, so a battery that can push 20 megawatts over four hours takes real pressure off during the most expensive hours. GM Darren Springer calls it by far the most significant battery storage on the city grid. Worth noting a similar Vergennes project stalled on community fire concerns, so Burlington moving unanimously stands out.

[Vermont faces an emergency blood shortage from summer donation lull](https://vtdigger.org/2026/07/16/vermont-faces-an-emergency-blood-shortage-from-summer-donation-lull/)

❝

*"By the time that you need the blood, you usually feel pretty crappy... And then right after you get the transfusion, there were days where I was like, 'I think I could run five miles,'" per VTDigger.*

The Red Cross declared a nationwide emergency blood shortage after the national supply fell 25 percent in June, and Vermont is feeling it, with about half of the state's 2,860 donation appointments over the next two weeks still unfilled. Only 3 percent of Americans donate in a given year. If you have been meaning to go, this is the week, donors who give by July 31 get a movie ticket out of it, and platelet donations happen at the permanent North Prospect Street site here in Burlington.

[Vermont Renaissance Faire styles redefine tradition](https://vtcommunitynews.org/2026/06/30/vermont-renaissance-faire-styles-redefine-tradition/)

❝

*"It's just a place where people can kind of be what they want to be, and nobody really even bats an eye about it," per Community News Service.*

A fun look inside the fashion side of the Vermont Renaissance Faire at the Champlain Valley Expo, where more than 70 small businesses and vendors lean into handmade and secondhand goods, including Milk Moth Vintage out of Burlington's Old North End. It is a fun inversion of the real Renaissance, when sumptuary laws dictated what each class could wear; the modern faire turns that on its head, making costume a form of pure self expression.

[CVU turf proposal clears hurdle](https://www.willistonobserver.com/community/schools/cvu-turf-proposal-clears-hurdle/article_64f3e60f-8fb4-446d-a3cd-6af2dec18a29.html)

❝

*"The claim that the field is 100 percent PFAS free is virtually impossible. I'm not convinced this field would even meet the legal standard required in Vermont now, and it will certainly be litigated," per the Williston Observer.*

The Champlain Valley School Board voted 8-3 to accept a donated artificial turf field at CVU, a gift led by Healthy Living CEO Eli Lesser-Goldsmith, over sustained opposition from Hinesburg residents worried about PFAS and microplastics. The district's own consultant found PFAS could reach town groundwater, though below federal drinking water standards. With a Williston legislator predicting a lawsuit under Vermont's new PFAS ban and the gift agreement still not public, the vote reads like the end of the beginning rather than the end.

[Vermont Conversation: Some joy, some tragedy. 2 Vermont authors on the struggles of rural living](https://vtdigger.org/2026/07/15/vermont-conversation-some-joy-some-tragedy-2-vermont-authors-on-the-struggles-of-rural-living/)

❝

*"Vermont is a place of multiplicity, that people come from all kinds of walks of life... some joyous, some tragedy. But between that, there's this sense of respect for the individual for what they're doing," per VTDigger.*

Two new novels put small town Vermont on the page. Hardwick's Brett Ann Stanciu sets "Call It Madness" in a fictional village based on Greensboro, where she works as town treasurer, and Corinth's Catherine Tudish builds "A Thousand Souls" around a village loosely modeled on Strafford. Bonus trivia for the playlist crowd, Tudish knew a young Noah Kahan in Strafford and says his curiosity stood out even at age 3. Two good candidates for the summer reading pile.

[Young People Want to Leave Vermont Over Affordability Concerns, Poll Finds](https://www.sevendaysvt.com/news/young-people-want-to-leave-vermont-over-affordability-concerns-poll-finds/)

❝

*"60 percent of residents between the ages of 18 and 34 saying they will 'likely' move away within the next five years," per Seven Days.*

A UNH poll of 981 Vermonters puts hard numbers on a familiar worry. Beyond the young resident figure, 86 percent of Vermonters overall find the state at least somewhat unaffordable, and the comparison stings, only 21 percent of young New Hampshirites say the same about leaving. The housing math underneath is the part to keep an eye on, Vermont needs an estimated 36,000 more homes and median prices have climbed roughly 9 percent a year since 2020. Definitely worth a full read.

[The two Democrats running for Chittenden County sheriff couldn't be more different](https://vtdigger.org/2026/07/13/the-two-democrats-running-for-chittenden-county-sheriff-couldnt-be-more-different/)

❝

*"There's so much potential in this office to be really useful for our community, but we're just letting all this potential rot on the vine," per VTDigger.*

The August 11 primary offers a genuine contrast. Incumbent Dan Gamelin, 65, is a 40 year law enforcement veteran from Winooski who manages 22 full time and nine part time deputies and defends the office's traditional role. Challenger Kevin Bloom, 32, owns a music studio, has no law enforcement background, and campaigns on not carrying out evictions for nonpayment of rent, though he says he would still enforce court orders, with a Ben Cohen endorsement in hand. The piece digs into the money too, from Gamelin's landlord donors to the legal 5 percent of contract revenue he pockets.

### Quick Hits

[GlobalFoundries and ECHO announce new partnership](https://www.mynbc5.com/article/globalfoundries-echo-stem-partnership/72795149)

The chipmaker and the waterfront science center signed a five year STEM education partnership that funds ECHO's traveling STEM truck, new exhibits and festivals, and its year long teacher institute. A nice follow-on from the earlier collaboration that built ECHO's current engineering space.

[New Vermont State Police Commander Rose Up Through the Ranks](https://www.sevendaysvt.com/news/new-vermont-state-police-commander-rose-up-through-the-ranks/)

Shawn Loan, a 19 year VSP veteran who came up through the Derby barracks and ran the Vermont Intelligence Center, takes over as colonel August 7, replacing the retiring Matthew Birmingham. He will oversee roughly 300 sworn troopers and says he wants the agency leaning on data to prevent crashes rather than just respond to them.

[South Burlington City Council signs off on settlement for Dorset rec path](https://www.vtcng.com/otherpapersbvt/news/municipal_matters/south-burlington-city-council-signs-off-on-settlement-for-dorset-rec-path/article_7dec5768-c532-4c1d-bc9d-94d87ef39e9b.html)

South Burlington will pay one holdout family $20,000 for easements, ending the eminent domain fight over the final piece of the Dorset Street shared use path from Old Cross Road to Sadie Lane. The section has been a city priority since 2016 and appeared in plans as far back as 1996, and with the final easement settled, construction is expected this year.

## Vermont Green FC: It’s the Playoffs!!

What a weekend for the Green. Start with the women, who took their unbeaten inaugural season all the way to the [USL W League national final in Seattle](https://vermontgreenfc.com/uslw-final-match-report-vermont-green-1-1-salmon-bay-fc/) and came agonizingly close. Louisa Thomsen's equalizer six minutes into stoppage time forced extra time against Salmon Bay FC, and after 120 scoreless minutes more it came down to penalties, where the Green fell 5-4. They finish as [national runners-up without losing a single match all season](https://vermontgreenfc.com/historic-vermont-green-season-ends-at-uslw-final-in-seattle/), a 9 win, 5 draw, 0 loss debut under 28 year old head coach Abby Carchio, with captain Chloe Allen Gorman playing every minute of every match, the first player in club history to do it. Some season.

The men, meanwhile, are steamrolling through the USL2 playoffs. After Friday's [4-0 win over Ironbound SC](https://vermontgreenfc.com/usl2-playoff-match-report-vermont-green-4-0-ironbound-sc/) at Virtue Field, they did it again Sunday, [4-0 over the Ocean City Nor'easters](https://vermontgreenfc.com/usl2-playoff-match-report-vermont-green-4-0-ocean-city-noreasters/). That's eight straight wins, a club record, 39 matches unbeaten overall, a sixth clean sheet of the summer for Seth Wilson, and Diego Rosas up to eight goals and nine assists. Next up is Asheville City in the Eastern Conference Final at the USL2 finals site in Texas next weekend, a matchup that mirrors the one the women won on their run.

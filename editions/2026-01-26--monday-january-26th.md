## Weather & Monday Update :

Bundle up, Burlington. We're in the thick of it now, with fine powdery snow continuing to fall through the evening hours. Expect another 2 to 4 inches to accumulate before things taper off overnight, so commutes home could be a slow go. Temperatures will hover in the teens this afternoon before dipping into the single digits tonight, flirting with zero. Tuesday brings a quieter start, though a few stray snow showers may pop up, particularly near the mountains, with only minor additional accumulation. The rest of the week settles into a pattern of mostly cloudy skies and persistent cold, with highs stuck in the teens and overnights hovering around zero through Friday. Keep the layers handy.

If civic engagement is your thing, tonight offers a double feature. The Burlington City Council meets at 6pm at Contois Auditorium, with public forum kicking off at 6:30pm. The [agenda](https://burlingtonvt.portal.civicclerk.com/event/8627/files/agenda/16017) is packed: resolutions on community dialogue around the Middle East conflict, a vote on whether to place the Apartheid Free Community Pledge on the Town Meeting ballot (for the third year running, and expected to be voted down again), and a proposed $0.05 increase to the Public Safety Tax to help address the city's projected $10 to $12 million budget gap. Meanwhile, over in South Burlington, local legislators are hosting their monthly [Legislative Forum](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/469481) at the South Burlington Library from 6 to 7:30pm, available in person or via Zoom. And if you'd rather dodge responsibility than discuss it, [Pickup Dodgeball](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/463462) at the Miller Center runs from 7 to 8:15pm. It's only $5 and no experience required. Also tonight, local rock band Mirage is filming a TV special at [LCATV](https://www.facebook.com/share/1CwxvedHJZ/) in Colchester at 6pm for the Havana Fairfax Connection series, and they're looking for audience members to fill the seats.

Tuesday brings a tasty lineup. Morning folks can swing by Zero Gravity on Pine Street for [Tech Tuesdays](https://www.meetup.com/vermont-technology-meetup/events/312767257/) [MEETUP] from 8 to 10am, a casual networking breakfast for founders, developers, and the tech curious. For dinner, head to American Flatbread on St. Paul Street from 4 to 10pm for the [CRAG VT Benefit Bake](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/469330), where $3.50 from every large pizza goes to support climbing access and land preservation across Vermont. Even if you've never touched a carabiner, CRAG land includes stretches of the Long Trail and habitat for falcons and other wildlife. That same evening, Local Motion hosts [EZ Breezy Bike Rides: Ignite the Night](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/469385) starting at 6pm from Steele Street, a casual, DJ accompanied group ride through Burlington's streets. Bundle up and keep the winter riding spirit alive. Parents of middle and high schoolers should mark down the [Healthy Media/Tech Use](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/468723) event at Hunt Middle School from 5:30 to 8pm, featuring BSD tech specialists and Howard Center counselors tackling the realities of raising kids in an always connected world. Dinner's included, and you can drop in for just the sessions that matter most. Runners can also meet up at Black Flannel in Essex for a [5K Run](https://www.meetup.com/getting-active-in-burlington/events/312615290/) [MEETUP] at 5:30pm (bring your lights, it'll be dark). The [Lakeside Open Mic](https://www.facebook.com/share/1AXopP8bwC/) at The Pickled Perch in Colchester starts at 7pm, with sign up opening at 6pm. And over in the New North End, the [Hot Dish Potluck](https://www.facebook.com/share/17r6xfxYy2/) at the Odd Fellows Hall (1416 North Ave) runs from 6:30 to 8:30pm. Bring a casserole or any cheesy baked delight to share. It's free, all ages, and substance free.

Wednesday keeps the momentum going. La Reprise on Pine Street hosts a [fundraiser for Stepping Stones Children's Center](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/467457) from 5 to 10pm, with a $75 prix fixe dinner and $25 going to support the South End nonprofit that's been serving families for over 35 years. Reservations available online. The [Wards 4 & 7 NPA Meeting](https://frontporchforum.com/calendar/events/469838) at the Miller Center (6 to 8pm, dinner at 6) will cover Town Meeting ballot items including the Tax Fairness Advisory Question, the Public Safety Tax increase, the REIB charter change, and the school budget. Mayor Emma Mulvaney Stanik will be there. For a more laid back Wednesday, there's a [Paint Party at Devil Takes a Holiday](https://www.eventbrite.com/e/paint-party-at-devil-takes-a-holiday-tickets-1980276932613) from 6 to 7:30pm. $35 gets you a guided painting session and one drink. It's 21+, no skill required, just good vibes and colorful canvases. Hoops enthusiasts can hit up [Pick-up Basketball](https://www.meetup.com/burlington-social-activites-group/events/313026844/) [MEETUP] with me at Gutterson Fieldhouse from 5:30 to 7:30pm ($10 day pass). I go every week anyway, so come out and join other UVM kids/community members. And at Higher Ground Ballroom, singer songwriter [Dean Johnson](https://highergroundmusic.com/events/dean-johnson/) plays a seated, all ages show touring his debut album on Saddle Creek, with Lily Seabird opening. Doors at 7:30pm, show at 8pm.

Thursday is stacked. [Vermont Stage](https://www.facebook.com/share/14Q8PgdyaPK/) opens Murder on the Orient Express at Main Street Landing at 7:30pm, Ken Ludwig's theatrical adaptation of the Agatha Christie classic. It runs through the weekend with shows Friday and Saturday as well, appropriate for ages 12 and up. The [Vermont Catamounts](https://www.facebook.com/share/1FK4mktHYR/) host UMBC at Patrick Gym at 7pm in an America East matchup, a good chance to see how the conference race is shaping up. Meanwhile, the Btown Brief crew will be meeting for [Trivia Night](https://www.meetup.com/burlington-social-activites-group/events/313026773/) [MEETUP] at Four Quarters Brewing in Winooski starts at 5:30pm (game at 6pm).

[Burlington City Council Will Vote Again on 'Apartheid-Free' Resolution](https://www.sevendaysvt.com/news/burlington-city-council-will-vote-again-on-apartheid-free-resolution/)

❝

"For the third straight year, Burlington city councilors will vote on whether to add a resolution to the Town Meeting Day ballot about supporting Palestinians and 'declaring ourselves an apartheid-free community,'" per Seven Days.

Tonight's vote comes after Council President Ben Traverse initially tried to keep the item off the agenda, citing safety concerns and the divisive atmosphere surrounding the issue. The city attorney ruled he couldn't block it. Democrats, who hold seven of 12 seats, have voted against similar measures the past two years and are expected to do so again. Also on the agenda is a separate resolution from Democratic councilors to fund community dialogue sessions through the Community Justice Center, an attempt to address the conflict's local impact without the ballot question.

[Vermont USCIS Employees Tapped to Help ICE Enforcement in Minnesota](https://vnews.com/2026/01/23/uscis-employees-reassigned-minnesota/)

❝

"At least seven U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services employees based in Vermont have been told they will be temporarily reassigned to Minnesota to help with expanding enforcement actions there," per VTDigger via the Valley News.

These workers typically process applications and check for immigration fraud. They've never been sent out of state or performed enforcement work before. Rep. Becca Balint called the move "outrageous," noting these are public servants who signed up to help people navigate the system, not conduct door to door enforcement. The reassignments come amid heightened tensions following the fatal shooting of a Minneapolis woman by an ICE agent earlier this month, which sparked protests including one in Williston that drew about 300 people; on top of the shooting we had over the weekend back in Minneapolis.

[Mothership Brewery Launches in Burlington; Broken Hearts Burger Closes in Fairlee](https://www.sevendaysvt.com/food-drink/foodnews/mothership-brewery-launches-in-burlington-broken-hearts-burger-closes-in-fairlee/)

❝

"The microbrewery at American Flatbread Burlington Hearth, where Zero Gravity Craft Brewery began in 2004, is now Mothership Brewery," per Seven Days.

Head brewer Destiny Saxon and cofounder Paul Sayler separated the brewpub at 115 St. Paul Street from Zero Gravity back in 2022, though the two still collaborate in limited fashion. Mothership works more closely now with Stowe's Idletyme Brewing, and its rotating tap list is only available at American Flatbread. A "hard launch celebration" is planned for around Earth Day. Meanwhile, up in the Upper Valley, Broken Hearts Burger in Fairlee closed January 10, with owner Matt Walker's Instagram now reading "On a hiatus or gone forever. Nobody's really sure."

[UVM Sociology Professor Enters Vermont State Senate Race](https://vtcynic.com/news/uvm-sociology-professor-enters-vermont-state-senate-race/)

❝

"What I know is that for too many Vermonters, the dream of a decent, affordable life is fading," per the Vermont Cynic.

Nikhil Goyal, an adjunct assistant professor at UVM and former senior policy advisor to Sen. Bernie Sanders, launched his campaign for the Chittenden Central seat as a Democrat/Progressive. His platform centers on property tax relief, education funding, healthcare costs, and affordable housing. Goyal has also pledged to accompany immigrants to court hearings, citizenship interviews, and ICE check ins, positioning himself as a vocal opponent of the Trump administration's immigration policies.

[Vermont High School Juniors Can Apply for a Free, Fast-Tracked Associate Degree](https://www.benningtonbanner.com/community-news/vermont-high-school-juniors-can-apply-for-a-free-fast-tracked-associate-degree/article_56d9e12c-dad3-4883-b095-58df4e047c99.html)

❝

"The Free Degree Promise builds on the state's Early College program to give students the chance to complete a free associate degree of their choosing just one year after high school graduation," per the Bennington Banner.

The program, available to current 10th, 11th, and 12th graders, has seen students graduate at twice the rate and in half the time compared to community college peers nationally. Since launching in 2022, CCV has tripled the number of low income students accessing Early College and persisting toward degrees. Vermont has New England's lowest rate of students continuing to college after high school at 40%, but students who participate in Early College continue at 80%. Applications are open now at [ccv.edu/freedegree](https://ccv.edu/freedegree).

[On the Beat: Lane Series Returns](https://www.sevendaysvt.com/music/musicnews/on-the-beat-lane-series-returns/)

❝

"The University of Vermont Lane Series, which launched in 1955 and is dedicated to bringing top-notch musical talent to the acoustically gorgeous UVM Recital Hall, has announced another season of incredible musicians," per Seven Days.

[Neighbors Sue Bagel Company](https://www.vtcng.com/otherpapersbvt/news/business_news/neighbors-sue-bagel-company/article_8dbbf350-dd67-423f-b31f-668ebd4a1022.html)

❝

"Two South Burlington residents have been in a years-long legal battle with their neighbor, Myer's Bagels on Shelburne Road, over what they say is 'noxious and potentially toxic' smoke produced from the company's wood fire oven," per The Other Paper.

Robert Payson and Kathy Brunette filed suit in 2024 alleging nuisance, trespass, and negligence after Myer's moved to Shelburne Road in 2023. A Superior Court judge issued a split decision in December, dismissing the trespass and negligence claims but allowing the nuisance claim to proceed to trial. Myer's installed a blower to address the issue, but the neighbors say problems persist. The parties are headed to mediation in February.

[Vermont: Bipartisan Lawmakers File Bill to Expand Marijuana Sales, Cut Excise Tax, and Allow Events](https://themarijuanaherald.com/2026/01/vermont-marijuana/)

❝

"A bipartisan group of six Vermont lawmakers has filed legislation that would make sweeping changes to the state's regulated marijuana market," per The Marijuana Herald.

Senate Bill 278 would eliminate THC concentration limits for flower, raise the cap for concentrates to 70%, and double the retail purchase limit to two ounces. It would also authorize cannabis events, delivery services, and on premises consumption alongside food service. Perhaps most notably for the industry, the bill would cut the state's cannabis excise tax from 14% to 10% and remove the requirement for pre approval of advertisements. Municipalities that haven't yet voted on allowing retailers would be required to put the question on the 2026 ballot.

[Radio Bean's Future in Flux as Owner Plans to Step Aside](https://www.sevendaysvt.com/arts-culture/radio-beans-future-in-flux-as-owner-plans-to-step-aside/)

❝

"The reality is that it's no longer possible for me to operate the business the way I have in the past with a family and two young kids," per Seven Days.

Lee Anderson, who founded Radio Bean 25 years ago, announced he's looking for new ownership for the North Winooski Avenue venue. His ideal scenario is a cooperative model where he stays on as a co-owner, though he's open to other arrangements. The club's financial struggles are "no secret," Anderson said, and he believes diversifying beyond drink and ticket sales is essential. Radio Bean remains open with months of shows booked, and Anderson hopes to have a definitive plan by summer. He's keenly aware of the venue's importance to Burlington's music scene, especially after Nectar's closed last year.

[3 Years After Act 76, Lawmakers Eye Vermont's Lagging Child Care Workforce](https://vtdigger.org/2026/01/21/3-years-after-act-76-lawmakers-eye-vermonts-lagging-child-care-workforce/)

❝

"We deserve a career with respect, longevity, value and fair compensation," said Jay Peak child care manager Shannon Edmonds-Folsom, per VTDigger.

Since Act 76 expanded the state's child care subsidy program in 2023, the industry has added 100 programs and 1,700 care slots. But workforce recruitment remains the primary constraint. Lawmakers are considering S.206, which would create a tiered state licensure system for child care providers. Advocates say it would establish a clearer career path, though some worry it could disadvantage workers who don't go through traditional degree programs. The committee heard testimony Wednesday on balancing higher standards with practical pathways for those already in the field.

[Beta Technologies Seeks to Pilot Medical Flights in Vermont This Year](https://www.vermontpublic.org/local-news/2026-01-22/beta-technologies-seeks-to-pilot-medical-flights-in-vermont-this-year)

❝

"Beta Technologies hopes a new Trump initiative will help the company more quickly bring its electric aircraft to market — beginning, perhaps, in Vermont," per Vermont Public.

The South Burlington firm has proposed partnering with helicopter operator Metro Aviation to move medical supplies between Vermont and northern New York, potentially launching later this year. The proposal responds to Trump's "Unleashing American Drone Dominance" executive order, which instructs the FAA to partner with states and electric aviation companies on demonstration projects. Beta CEO Kyle Clark told Congress the initiative could advance the industry by a year. Metro Aviation, which already provides medical transport at UVM Medical Center, plans to purchase at least 20 of Beta's aircraft with delivery expected in 2028.

[Sweetwaters to Reopen on Burlington's Church Street](https://www.sevendaysvt.com/food-drink/foodnews/sweetwaters-to-reopen-on-burlingtons-church-street/)

❝

"Sweetwaters is making a comeback," per Seven Days.

David Melincoff, who owned the downtown fixture for half of its 41 year run, is working with investors and former executive chef Jessee Lawyer to reopen the 120 Church Street restaurant by late April. Sweetwaters closed in 2022 to make way for Pascolo Ristorante, which recently moved back to its original location at 83 Church Street, leaving the space vacant. Lawyer, who cooked at Sweetwaters for nine years, plans to tighten the menu while keeping fan favorites like the cornflake crusted chicken fingers and Shirt Sleeve wrap. He's also bringing back the smoker for wings, pulled pork, and brisket.

[UVM's Center for Community News Announces New Hires and Promotions](https://vermontbiz.com/news/2026/january/23/uvms-center-community-news-announces-new-hires-and-promotions)

❝

"CCN is an example of what's possible when we match student passions with faculty expertise and civic needs," said Dean William Falls, per Vermont Business Magazine.

The Center for Community News announced three key appointments: Courtney Lamdin, formerly of Seven Days, joins as Community News Service editor; Scott Finn's role expands to UVM's first lecturer of journalism and democracy; and Sima Bhowmik has been hired as research assistant professor after serving as CCN's first post-doctoral fellow. The organization's Vermont newsroom now produces roughly 400 original stories annually for partner outlets across the state, with more than 100 students participating each year as reporters, researchers, and interns.

## UVM Athletics: Basketball and Women's Hockey Sweep Weekend; Records Fall in Pool

The Vermont men’s basketball team secured a 62-52 road victory over Bryant on Saturday afternoon. [TJ Hurley led the offense with 26 points](https://uvmathletics.com/news/2026/1/24/mens-basketball-mens-basketball-defeats-bryant-62-52-on-the-road-saturday-afternoon.aspx), shooting 8-for-16 from the field, marking his second consecutive game with at least 26 points. Gus Yalden contributed 14 points as the Catamounts held a 30-17 lead at halftime and maintained control through the second half.

At Patrick Gymnasium, the women’s basketball team extended its home winning streak against Bryant to seven games with a [71-53 victory](https://uvmathletics.com/news/2026/1/24/womens-basketball-womens-basketball-extends-home-winning-streak-against-bryant-in-71-53-victory.aspx). Jadyn Weltz scored a season-high 21 points, while Keira Hanson added 12 points. Vermont shot 53.3% from three-point range and held the Bulldogs to 39.3% shooting from the field.

On the ice, the women’s hockey team completed a weekend sweep of Boston University. On Saturday, [Stella Retrum scored two goals](https://uvmathletics.com/news/2026/1/24/womens-ice-hockey-womens-hockey-defeats-boston-university-2-1-on-saturday-retrum-and-mespl-de-play-in-100th-career-game.aspx) in her 100th career game to lift Vermont to a 2-1 win. Goaltender Zoe Cliche made 27 saves to secure the victory. This followed a [5-2 win on Friday](https://uvmathletics.com/news/2026/1/23/womens-ice-hockey-womens-hockey-special-teams-plays-pivotal-role-in-5-2-victory-over-boston-university-on-friday-night.aspx) where special teams proved pivotal; the Catamounts scored three power-play goals and one shorthanded goal. Darci Matson netted her first goal as a Catamount in the Friday contest.

The men’s hockey team dropped a pair of games to UMass at Gutterson Fieldhouse. In a defensive battle on Saturday, [UMass scored a late winner](https://uvmathletics.com/news/2026/1/24/mens-ice-hockey-late-winner-lifts-umass-past-vermont-saturday.aspx) with 6:20 remaining to take a 1-0 decision, despite a strong performance from Vermont goaltender Aiden Wright. On Friday, UMass goaltender Michael Hrabal made 42 saves to [shut out the Catamounts 4-0](https://uvmathletics.com/news/2026/1/23/mens-ice-hockey-hrabal-leads-umass-past-vermont-friday-night.aspx).

In Olympic sports, the swimming and diving team [defeated Siena 167-114](https://uvmathletics.com/news/2026/1/24/womens-swimming-and-diving-six-pool-records-fall-as-catamounts-defeat-siena-on-senior-day.aspx) on Senior Day, setting six new pool records at Forbush Natatorium. Records fell in the 200 Yard Medley Relay, 100 Back, 100 Breast, 400 IM, 50 Free, and 200 Free Relay.

The Nordic skiing team wrapped up the [Colby Carnival](https://uvmathletics.com/news/2026/1/24/nordic-skiing-wraps-up-colby-carnival-on-saturday-bailey-rizio-and-brewster-earn-podium-appearances.aspx) with three podium finishes on Saturday. Fin Bailey earned his first career win in the 7.5k CL Int., while Luke Rizio placed second. Haley Brewster finished third for the women’s team, helping the squad to a second-place overall finish at the carnival.

Finally, the track and field team competed at the [MIT Gordon Kelly Invitational](https://uvmathletics.com/news/2026/1/24/womens-track-field-beal-wins-again-in-busy-meet-at-mit.aspx), where Alex Beal won the 1000m for her fourth straight victory. Erin Carty added a win in the 200m, and Kyle Hoffman led a 1-2 Vermont finish in the men's 400m.
